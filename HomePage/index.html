The burning of fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, releases pollutants into the air.

More than 18 million people live in counties that got an F for all three air pollution measures in “State of the Air” 2023.

Air pollution can trigger asthma attacks, harm lung development in children, and increase risk of lung cancer.

Burning fossil fuels is also the largest single source of global greenhouse gas emissions. (23% of global greenhouse gas emissions)

From 1990 to 2019, the total warming effect from greenhouse gasses added by humans to the Earth’s atmosphere increased by 45 percent.

Each oil rig uses between 1.5 million to 16 million gallons of water, and creates toxic wastewater, often containing substances like arsenic, lead, and mercury that can contaminate groundwater and drinking water.

The world’s largest oil spill was Deepwater Horizon in the Gulf of Mexico in 2010. The US Government estimates that 700,000 tons of oil were released.
