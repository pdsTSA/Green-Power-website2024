Green Power Partnership

The EPA's Green Power Partnership promotes voluntary involvement in the green power market by providing information, assistance, and acknowledgment to companies employing green power. These companies commit to using green power for a portion or all of their yearly electricity consumption. Presently, partners' green power usage represents close to 37% of the U.S. voluntary green power market (excluding hydropower). Since its establishment in 2001, the program has aided in preventing almost 280 million metric tons of greenhouse gas emissions. Learn more at epa.gov/greenpower.

SmartWay Transport Partnership

SmartWay collaborates with thousands of freight industry participants to promote advanced fuel-saving practices. It's widely embraced by leading companies across various sectors, resulting in substantial greenhouse gas emission reductions and cost savings totaling over $52 billion since 2004. Learn more at epa.gov/smartway.

Pennsylvania's Methane Emissions Reduction Partnership Programs

Pennsylvania's Methane Emissions Reduction Partnership Programs encourage the efficient recovery and utilization of methane from vital sectors like agriculture, coal mining, municipal solid waste, and the oil and gas industry. Through initiatives such as AgSTAR, Coalbed Methane Outreach Program, Landfill Methane Outreach Program, Natural Gas STAR, and Methane Challenge, these programs have collectively averted nearly 1.6 billion metric tons of greenhouse gas emissions and resulted in approximately $10 billion in cost savings. Learn more at epa.gov/energy/clean-energy-programs, epa.gov/natural-gas-star-program, and epa.gov/cmop.
